Week 1/10: Summer Goals

Friday, June 8, 2018

Hello! This is my second summer working with CRA's DREU program. Honestly, I'd say that last year was the zenith of my college experience (so far) by introducing me to research. I'm hoping that this summer is even better than the last... there's a lot of things that I want to accomplish.

List of goals for the summer

  • Start learning machine learning techniques and incorporate them into my work. This goal is far overdue for me.
  • Become more comfortable digging into the theory behind the systems I'm working with. Last year I got caught up trying to get the systems I was working with to actually work, not so much paying attention to the algoritems behind the systems. It's understandable because everything was really new to me, but I want to have a stronger sense of direction this summer. I feel like having this theoretical knowledge will also help me as I get to know more of the field I work in.
  • Along with the previous note, become more comfortable reading technical papers. The previous summer I felt like I read papers by very carefully reading through each paragraph, looking up terms and facts that I wasn't familiar with as I went, scribbling down tonnes of note. Sure, I came out of the process knowing more I did at the beginning, but it's not nearly as efficient as I want to be!
  • Spend more time in the lab and get to know more researchers! It would be nice to have a community of people to do work with.

On a different note, this week I learned how to soldier! Pictured below are a bunch of microbugs that I helped prepare. They're little robots that run towards the nearest source of light. All I did was soldier on the transistors for the kids since they frequently mess up that step. It's something small, but it was oddly refreshing working with something actually tangible!

Joan Zheng, 2018.
